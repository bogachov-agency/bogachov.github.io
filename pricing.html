Cats "talk" to humans but not to each other

Cats don’t use meowing to communicate with other adult cats — it’s a "language" they develop specifically for interacting with humans. Many cat owners notice their pets meow differently depending on the situation.

Learn more ->
They sleep most of their lives

Cats spend about 70% of their lives sleeping. This means a 12-year-old cat has been awake for only about 4 years! Sleep helps them recover and conserve energy for hunting (or playing).

Learn more ->
Cats can "hear ultrasound"

Cats’ ears are so sensitive that they can pick up ultrasonic sounds emitted by mice or bats. This makes them incredibly effective hunters.

Learn more ->
Whiskers are their "sensors"

A cat’s whiskers aren’t just for decoration — they’re essential sensory tools. They help cats determine whether they can fit through narrow spaces and detect air movements, which reveal obstacles or moving objects, even in complete darkness.

Learn more ->
Unique "nose print"

A cat’s nose is as unique as a human fingerprint. The pattern on a cat's nose is individual to each cat and, in theory, could be used for identification.
